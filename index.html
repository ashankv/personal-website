Finalist of Conrad Innovation Challenge (2015-2016)

Part of a two person team which designed a low cost exoskeleton prototype. Used thin and coiled fishing line to design and artificial muscle that contracts upon heating and expands upon cooling. Designed and implemented Arduino Uno software to read electromyography (EMG) signals and control the muscle arm. Spent 1 week at NASA, Florida to attend seminars and showcase the project. Received an appreciation letter from California Congressman Mike Honda.

2nd Place at Alameda County Science and Engineering Fair

Part of two person team which participated in the Alameda County Science and Engineering Fair and was awarded 2nd place in the Energy, Environmental and Earth Sciences category. The prototype provides a cheap, alternative way of harnessing the electrical energy generated by graphene strips when exposed to an ionic fluid.
